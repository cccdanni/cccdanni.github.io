About Me

Hello! I am Danni Chen (陈丹妮). Welcome to my personal website!

I am now a Post-doctoral Fellow under the supervision of Dr. Xiaoqing Hu (胡晓晴博士) at the Social and Cognitive Neuroscience Lab, Department of Psychology, The University of Hong Kong (香港大学). I obtained my Ph.D. in Psychology from HKU (2025) and Bachor of Science in Psychology (2019) from East China Normal University(华东师范大学), where I worked with Prof. Xianchun Li (李先春教授) and Prof. Lin Li (李林教授).

I work at the intersection of sleep, social cognitive neuroscience, and memory-guided decision-making. My research focuses on:
(1) how sleep supports complex learning, such as social and affective learning;
(2) how memory influences our dynamic decision-making.
Together, I aim to understand how sleep and memory interact to shape our adaptive behaviors.

I utilize a variety of tools and methodologies, including EEG, eyetracking, mousetracking, and computational modeling. Additionally, I am eager to integrate more computational social science and neuroscience approaches into my work.

For details and my publication, please refer to Research and Publication tab. Please feel free to reach me out via dnchen@connect.hku.hk if you have any questions, or any potential collaboration.

July 17 2024


Our paper Modulating social learning-induced evaluation updating during human sleep is now available at npj Science of Learning. This is my first first-authored sleep and TMR research. In this paper, we examined whether TMR can reactivate pre-sleep social learning to further influence evaluation updatings. The results showed YES, and the overnight evaluation updating was related to cue-elicited delta-theta power and N2 sleep spindle density that are related to sleep-based memory consolidation.
