    3. This is my millestone paper on Sleep and Social Learning. Specifically, we found that social learning-induced evaluation updating can be modulated during human sleep, and the modulation is associated with the cue-elicited delta-theta power and overnight N2 sleep spindle, which provides a new perspective on the role of sleep in social learning.
